1) Charlie — 30 × 40 in

Medium: Acrylic on presentation panel, mounted

Starting Bid: $750

A vibrant, textured homage to one of cinema’s greatest icons, this expressive portrait captures the timeless charm and melancholy of early film history. Thick impasto strokes and a nostalgic palette evoke both humor and humanity — a reflection on the spirit of performance and perseverance that transcends the silent screen. Painted in Kenneth J. Lewis Sr.’s signature bold color blocking and sculptural brushwork, Charlie bridges classic imagery with a contemporary painterly edge.

2) My Luggage My Life — 40 × 48 in

Medium: Acrylic and collage on canvas

Starting Bid: $1,200

A powerful mixed-media work that chronicles the personal and spiritual journey of self-discovery. Through painted suitcases, affirmations, and layered textures, this piece invites viewers into the process of unpacking identity, healing wounds, and embracing transformation. Text fragments like “I Am,” “Reclaim,” and “Becoming” form a visual map of resilience — a testament to survival and renewal. Each color, word, and symbol is an emotional checkpoint in the artist’s evolving story.

3) Basquiat Portrait 6 (Framed) — ~24 × 36 in

Medium: Acrylic on Luan board, custom lime-green frame

Starting Bid: $600

A raw and expressive tribute to the spirit of rebellion and genius, Basquiat Portrait 6 pulses with energy and urban rhythm. With gestural brushwork, graphic marks, and bright graffiti-inspired color, this portrait captures the fearless creativity and street-edge innovation that reshaped modern art. The hand-painted lime frame acts as a bold extension of the work, turning the entire piece into a statement of individuality and power.

4) Spa Day — 12 × 12 in (art); 21½ × 21½ in framed

Medium: Acrylic on architectural panel, white traditional frame

Starting Bid: $350

An abstract meditation in turquoise, ochre, and cream, Spa Day channels a sense of peace and reflection through rhythmic line and texture. The composition flows like water, offering a visual pause — a moment of calm and restoration within a structured balance of form and color. Framed elegantly in white, this work radiates freshness and serenity, ideal for intimate spaces or as part of a modern collection.

5) Laundry Day — 12 × 12 in (art); 21½ × 21½ in framed

Medium: Acrylic on architectural panel, white traditional frame

Starting Bid: $350

Dynamic and layered, Laundry Day translates the routine of everyday life into abstraction. Blocks of sky blue, ochre, and cream merge with expressive lines that evoke movement, rhythm, and renewal — like freshly washed fabric fluttering in sunlight. A joyful study in texture and color balance, framed for contemporary display.
